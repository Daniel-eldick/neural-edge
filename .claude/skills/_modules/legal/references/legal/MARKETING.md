# Marketing, Advertising & Anti-Spam Compliance

## Applicability

Applies to ALL marketing activities: landing pages, SEO content, competitor comparisons, email marketing, SMS, push notifications, social media.

- **US**: FTC Act §5 (unfair/deceptive practices), Lanham Act (competitor claims), CAN-SPAM (email)
- **Canada**: CASL (anti-spam — $10M fines, strictest globally)
- **EU**: Unfair Commercial Practices Directive (UCPD), ePrivacy Directive
- **UK**: Consumer Protection from Unfair Trading Regulations 2008, PECR
- **Australia**: Australian Consumer Law (ACL), Spam Act 2003

## Key Requirements

### Truth in Advertising

1. **ALL claims must be truthful and substantiated** (FTC Act §5)
2. **Statistics must be verifiable and current** — "15,000+ orders" must match reality
3. **Competitor comparisons must be factual, fair, not misleading** (FTC Guides, EU UCPD Art. 6)
4. **Puffery vs specific claims** — "best" is puffery, "$0 monthly fee" is a specific claim needing proof
5. **Pricing claims must reflect actual pricing** — no bait-and-switch
6. **Testimonials/social proof must be genuine** (FTC Endorsement Guides)
7. **Disclaimers must be clear and conspicuous** — not hidden in fine print
8. **EU comparative advertising**: allowed if objective, verifiable, not misleading, not denigrating
9. **"Free" claims** — must truly be free, disclose all conditions
10. **Environmental claims** must be substantiated (FTC Green Guides)

### Anti-Spam (Email, SMS, Push)

11. **CAN-SPAM (US)**: opt-out within 10 business days, physical address, clear sender, no deceptive subjects. Order confirmations EXEMPT. Marketing NOT exempt.
12. **CASL (Canada)**: express consent REQUIRED before marketing. Implied consent only for existing business relationships (2 years). $10M fines.
13. **GDPR (EU)**: consent required for direct marketing.
14. **PECR (UK)**: soft opt-in for existing customers (similar products only).
15. **Push notifications**: treated as marketing — need consent.
16. **Transactional vs marketing**: order confirmations = exempt. Promotions, loyalty updates = requires consent.

### Competitor Comparison Pages

17. Competitor pricing must be **verifiable and dated**
18. Feature comparisons must be **accurate at time of publication**
19. Must **acknowledge where competitors are stronger** (no one-sided comparisons)
20. **Review cycle**: update or remove stale comparisons every 6 months
21. **No trademark misuse** — can use names for comparison, not suggesting endorsement
22. **Search ads**: can bid on competitor keywords but ad copy must identify [Your Platform]

## [Your Platform]-Specific Implications

- **5 competitor comparison pages** need quarterly accuracy review ([your-platform]-vs-toast, [your-platform]-vs-square, etc.)
- **StatsBar "15,000+ orders"** vs actual ~3,312 — **active violation** under FTC Act §5 and EU UCPD
- **Social proof text** must be genuine ("Used by businesses daily")
- **Pricing comparison tables** must reflect current competitor pricing
- **FAQ answers are advertising claims** — must be accurate
- **Pain points** ("Toast hardware starts at $799") need sources and dates
- **WhatsApp FAB** is a marketing channel — CASL/GDPR implications
- **WaitlistForm.tsx** needs explicit consent language for marketing emails
- **Loyalty program marketing** must comply with CASL if targeting Canadian users

## Penalties

- **FTC**: injunctions, fines, corrective advertising orders
- **Lanham Act**: competitor lawsuits for false advertising
- **CAN-SPAM**: up to $51,744/violation
- **CASL**: up to $10M (corporate), $1M (individual)
- **EU UCPD**: member state enforcement
