# Consumer Protection

## Applicability

Consumer protection laws apply to B2C transactions. [Your Platform] facilitates business-to-consumer orders.

- **EU**: Consumer Rights Directive (CRD) 2011/83/EU, Digital Content Directive, Digital Services Act (DSA)
- **UK**: Consumer Rights Act 2015, Consumer Contracts Regulations 2013, DMCCA 2024
- **US**: FTC Act §5, state consumer protection laws (vary by state)
- **Canada**: Consumer Protection Act (varies by province)
- **Australia**: Australian Consumer Law (ACL)

**Key question**: Is [Your Platform] the seller or the platform? Generally: tenants are sellers, [Your Platform] is the platform. But platform liability is increasing globally (EU DSA, DMCCA 2024).

## Key Requirements

### 1. Pre-Contractual Information
Price, description, delivery time must be shown BEFORE order confirmation. Total including taxes and delivery fees.

### 2. Order Confirmation
Written confirmation of order details (email or on-screen). This is a legal requirement, not just UX.

### 3. Right of Withdrawal
14 days in EU — BUT **perishable goods (food) are EXEMPT** (CRD Art. 16). Good news for tenants.

### 4. Refund Obligations
Food orders generally non-refundable, but **defective/wrong orders must be refunded** (quality guarantee applies regardless of perishability).

### 5. Pricing Transparency
Total price including ALL taxes, delivery fees, service charges. No hidden charges. No drip pricing.

### 6. Cancellation Policy
Clear policy: when can customer cancel? Before preparation? After? Platform should provide guidance to restaurant tenants.

### 7. Delivery Promises
If delivery time is stated, it can be legally binding. Be careful with time estimates.

### 8. Digital Content Quality (UK CRA 2015)
SaaS must be: satisfactory quality, fit for purpose, as described. Features promised in marketing must be delivered.

### 9. Unfair Contract Terms
ToS cannot waive statutory consumer rights. Blanket liability exclusions may be unenforceable. One-sided termination clauses are risky.

Accessible complaint mechanism required. <!-- FILL: document your current complaint channels (e.g., email, chat, phone) -->. Should be documented in ToS.

### 11. Platform Liability (EU DSA)
If operating in EU: transparency obligations, notice-and-action for illegal content, reporting obligations.

### 12. Subscription Rules (UK DMCCA 2024)
From April 2025: pre-contract info, cooling-off periods, renewal reminders, cancellation must be as easy as sign-up. No dark patterns.

## [Your Platform]-Specific Implications

- Food = perishable = exempt from 14-day withdrawal (reduces refund burden)
- Order confirmation page IS a legal requirement — invest in its quality
- Pricing must show total including fees BEFORE checkout button
- ToS cannot override statutory rights — unfair terms are void
- Tenant tenants set cancellation policies — platform should template/guide
- EU DSA applies if serving EU users — transparency obligations
- Subscription plans need renewal reminders and easy cancellation (DMCCA)
- Complaint mechanism should be documented in ToS with specific channels listed

## Penalties

- **EU CRD**: member state enforcement, fines vary
- **UK CRA**: consumer claims, CMA enforcement
- **UK DMCCA**: up to 10% global turnover, CMA direct enforcement
- **FTC**: injunctions, fines
- **ACL Australia**: up to AUD $10M per contravention
- Class actions possible in US/Australia
